Introduction

Welcome to methodological presentation of my thesis for the master of Marketing Analytics and Data Science at the University of Groningen, Faculty of Economics and Business. It is split up into several pages. The links to them are located to the left. The first pages are about exploring and cleansing the 'internal' dataset. I refer to it as such because it is the dataset that was initially assigned by the university. It is a public dataset published on Kaggle. It contains review and order data of approximately 100k Brazilian shoppers at an online marketplace called Olist.

The Olist dataset was enhanced with data of the 2010 population census in Brazil. Combining both sets was one of the main challenges of the project. The bulk of the project was done in R (version 4.03). Lemmatization was done using Python (version 3.8) because of my preference for spaCy. After the fact I discovered that it could also be done from R using the 'spacyr' package. For those in a similar situation it would probably be easier to just use that instead. Myself, I didn't mind it too much, it was a nice change of scenery. Analyses reported in the thesis were done in STATA (version 16). I coded on several OS's but in the end, stuck with Windows 10 64-bit. This might make a difference in the second part where Windows seems to represent Latincharacters by default differently to Unix systems.
